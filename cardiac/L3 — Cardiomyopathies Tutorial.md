## Case 1 — DCM

### Q: The DCM stem in one sentence.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

25-year-old man, palpitations + syncope, Holter = 6h fast AF in 24h, TTE = inferolateral hypokinesia, **CMR confirms DCM → suspected HF**.

</details>

### Q: Applying the 4-step move — what does this stem point to?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. **Young adult + arrhythmic symptom** → cardiomyopathy, myocarditis, ARVC (not AS, not senile MR)
2. **Syncope + AF in <30** → abnormal atrial substrate → structural disease
3. **Holter → electrical; TTE → structural; CMR → confirms DCM**
4. Stem points to management + prognosis + L/R HF discrimination

🟦 🧠 Young-onset AF is structural until proven otherwise.

</details>

### Q: Define DCM (tutorial-expanded).

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Dilated chambers + thin walls + reduced contractility → systolic dysfunction.**

Echo: dilated LV, EF <50%, **global hypokinesis**.

🟥 ⚠️ Trap: the stem says **inferolateral** hypokinesia (regional) — more suggestive of ischaemic CM. CMR overrides in this case. But regional vs global is a key discriminator in SBAs.

- Regional hypokinesis = ischaemic
- Global hypokinesis = DCM

</details>

### Q: Causes of DCM — the exam list.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

- Idiopathic, genetic (~1/3)
- **Alcohol** (often biventricular)
- Cardiotoxic **chemotherapy** (anthracyclines)
- **Peripartum cardiomyopathy**
- Viral myocarditis
- Tachycardia-related (may be reversible)
- Thyroid, muscular dystrophies

</details>

### Q: Map aetiology to exam stem patterns.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

- Long heavy drinker → alcoholic DCM
- Postpartum <5 months → peripartum CM
- Breast cancer survivor + prior anthracyclines → chemo DCM
- Recent flu-like illness + new HF → viral myocarditis → DCM
- Prolonged uncontrolled tachycardia → tachycardia-mediated CM

</details>

### Q: DCM first-line HF drug per ESC 2023.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

🟨 ⭐ **SGLT2 inhibitors (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin) — Class I first-line.**

Before ACEi unless contraindicated (e.g. acute infection). Established since 2021, reinforced ESC 2023.

</details>

### Q: Full DCM management ladder.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

- **SGLT2i** (Class I first-line)
- ACEi / ARNI / ARB
- β-blocker
- MRA (spironolactone, eplerenone)
- Diuretics (symptom relief only)
- Anticoag for AF (standard CHA₂DS₂-VASc)
- Devices: CRT-P, CRT-ICD, LVAD, transplant

</details>

📎 Figure L3.3 — ESC 2023 HFmrEF + HFpEF algorithms (SGLT2i Class I).

### Q: DCM prognosis — what should this 25-year-old expect?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

- HF hospitalisations
- Cardiac arrhythmias (AF, VT)
- **SCD from ventricular arrhythmia**
- Reduced survival (Hollander 2012 KM curve — markedly reduced 1-year transplant-free survival)

Drives ICD decisions in advanced DCM with low EF.

</details>

### Q: Left vs right HF signs — how to tell them apart.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

- **Left HF** → **pulmonary oedema** (crackles, dyspnoea, orthopnoea, PND, pink frothy sputum)
- **Right HF** → **peripheral oedema** (ankle swelling, **↑ JVP**, hepatomegaly, ascites)

🟦 🧠 Lung vs leg.

Alcoholic DCM often → biventricular HF.

</details>

## Case 2 — HCM

### Q: The HCM stem in one sentence.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

20-year-old man dies suddenly. Autopsy: **hypertrophied IVS + LVH**. FH: father on ICD for VT/syncope at 50 (HCM, **no genetic testing or family screening**). Paternal grandfather died suddenly at 49.

</details>

### Q: Applying the 4-step move — what is this stem really testing?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

1. Young man + sudden death → HCM, ARVC, channelopathies, coronary anomalies
2. Autopsy IVS + LVH + 3-generation early sudden death → **autosomal dominant heritable CM**
3. Asymmetric septal hypertrophy → classic HCM morphology
4. Stem tests two things: **cascade screening failure** + **mechanism of SCD in HCM**

🟦 🧠 Multigenerational SCD <50 = heritable cardiac disease; first-degree relatives MUST be screened.

</details>

### Q: Define HCM + the critical phrase.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Genetic disorder characterised by LV hypertrophy not solely explained by abnormal loading conditions.**

🟥 ⚠️ "Not solely explained by abnormal loading conditions" is the exam-defining phrase. If LVH is due to AS or HTN, treating the load reverses it — that's not HCM.

Prevalence 1:500 UK. Mostly autosomal dominant.

</details>

### Q: HCM mutation classes — missense vs frameshift.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

- **Missense** — single amino acid change → **gain of function**: **MYH7** (β-myosin HC), **TNNT2** (troponin T)
- **Frameshift** — insertion/deletion → **truncated protein**: **MYBPC3** (myosin binding protein C)

Net effect: **sarcomere hypercontractility**.

MYH7 + MYBPC3 cover most HCM.

</details>

### Q: Why does HCM cause sudden cardiac death?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

```
Gene mutations
      │
      ▼
LV thickening + myocyte DISARRAY + patchy fibrosis
      │
      ▼
Disorganised myocytes disrupt signal conduction
      │
      ▼
Ventricular arrhythmias (re-entrant VT/VF)
      │
      ▼
Sudden cardiac death
```

Histology: **diffuse interstitial fibrosis + focal replacement fibrosis**. Both seen on CMR with gadolinium; extent predicts arrhythmic risk.

</details>

📎 Figure L3.4 — HCM pathophysiology to SCD + histology.

### Q: Define LVOTO and name its 2 causes in HCM.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**LVOTO = obstruction of blood flowing OUT of the LV.**

Causes in HCM:
1. **Septal and/or papillary muscle hypertrophy** (sarcomere mutation-driven)
2. **MV abnormalities** → leaflet elongation + **SAM (systolic anterior motion)**

</details>

### Q: SAM — describe the mechanism in a sentence.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

🟦 🧠 Hypertrophied papillary muscles are displaced anteriorly; chordae are slack; MV leaflets are elongated. During systole, the anterior MV leaflet gets **pulled toward the hypertrophied septum** by Venturi effect → **touches the septum** → blocks LV outflow AND leaks backward → **LVOTO + functional MR**.

🟨 ⭐ Echo: "SAM with septal contact" on parasternal long axis. Asymmetric septum (e.g. IVS 21 mm, posterior wall 13 mm).

</details>

📎 Figure L3.5 — Asymmetric HCM echo + SAM illustration (diastole vs systole).

### Q: The HCM diagnostic threshold — and when it drops.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

🟥 ⚠️ Adults: **≥15 mm** wall thickness in ≥1 LV myocardial segment in diastole.

Drops to **≥13 mm** if ANY of:
- Family history
- LVOTO
- ECG abnormalities

Children: >2 SD above BSA-predicted mean.

Miss this and you'll mark "no HCM" on a stem designed to test exactly this.

</details>

### Q: Full HCM diagnostic workup.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

- **TTE** (first-line) — wall thickness, LVOTO, SAM
- **ECG** — LVH, LA enlargement, deep Q waves
- **Cardiac MRI** — gold standard; gadolinium contrast detects fibrosis
- **Holter / event monitor** — detect VT, AF
- **Exercise/stress test** — provoke LVOTO
- **Genetic testing** — patient **+ first-degree relatives**

Plus: NT-proBNP; exclude other LVH causes (HTN, AS, amyloidosis).

</details>

### Q: HCM — what to give, what to avoid.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Give:**
- **Non-vasodilating β-blockers** (first-line)
- **Non-DHP CCBs** (verapamil, diltiazem)
- Disopyramide; myosin inhibitors (mavacamten)

🟥 ⚠️ **Avoid pure vasodilators** (ACEi in purely obstructive phase, DHP CCBs, nitrates) — ↓ afterload → smaller LV → **worse LVOTO**.

</details>

### Q: Septal reduction therapy — when and how?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Indication:** symptomatic obstructive HCM refractory to maximal medical therapy.

Options:
- **Surgical myectomy** — remove thickened septum
- **Alcohol septal ablation (PTSMA)** — alcohol into septal LAD branch → controlled necrosis → septum thins over months
- Pacing in selected cases

</details>

📎 Figure L3.6 — HCM management algorithms (JCS/JHFS 2018 + NRDP 2025).

### Q: AF in HCM — the anticoagulation rule.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

🟥 ⚠️ **Anticoagulate regardless of CHA₂DS₂-VASc score.**

Any AF in HCM = anticoagulate. The hypertrophied/fibrotic LA is inherently thrombogenic.

Single highest-yield HCM management trap.

</details>

### Q: The teaching point embedded in the HCM case.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

🟨 ⭐ **Cascade screening failure.** Father had HCM but no genetic testing or family screening requested → 20-year-old son's HCM never identified → preventable sudden death.

**Rule:** HCM is autosomal dominant. First-degree relatives must be offered **clinical screening (echo + ECG) + genetic testing**.

</details>

## SBA block (integrated)

### Q: SBA — 32-year-old woman, dyspnoea 4 months post-partum, no prior cardiac history, TTE: dilated LV, EF 28%, global hypokinesis. Most likely diagnosis?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Peripartum cardiomyopathy.** DCM phenotype within 5 months postpartum + no prior cardiac history.

Counsel: future pregnancies carry real recurrence risk.

</details>

### Q: SBA — 45-year-old with HCM + new AF, CHA₂DS₂-VASc 1. Anticoagulation?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Full anticoagulation regardless of CHA₂DS₂-VASc.**

Most tested HCM management trap.

</details>

### Q: SBA — 35-year-old marathon runner, FH of SCD, TTE wall thickness 13 mm at IVS, ECG deep Q waves + T-wave inversion, MYH7 mutation. Does he meet HCM criteria?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Yes.** 15 mm threshold drops to **13 mm** when FH or ECG abnormalities are present. He has both. MYH7 is the classic missense gain-of-function HCM mutation.

</details>

### Q: SBA — 60-year-old, idiopathic DCM, LVEF 38%, already on furosemide. Next drug per ESC 2023?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Dapagliflozin (SGLT2i).** Class I first-line ahead of ACEi.

</details>

### Q: SBA — 50-year-old, HCM, LVOT gradient 60 mmHg, exertional syncope on max β-blocker, IVS 24 mm, moderate SAM-driven MR. Next step?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Septal reduction therapy (myectomy or alcohol septal ablation).**

- Not ACEi / DHP CCB — vasodilators worsen LVOTO
- Not MV replacement — MR is functional (SAM) and usually resolves with septal reduction
- Not more β-blocker — already failed

</details>

## Reusable reasoning pattern

### Q: The transferable template for any CM / HF stem.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

🟦 🧠 Sequence:
1. Age + context → differential shortlist
2. Symptom triad / FH pattern → mechanism
3. TTE first → CMR for detail / fibrosis / aetiology hints

5. Screen + flag — heritable CMs need first-degree screening; HCM + AF needs score-independent anticoag
