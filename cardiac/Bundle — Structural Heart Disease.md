<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

## Convergence 1 — Heart Failure

### Q: How does each of the 6 diseases converge on heart failure?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

| Upstream | Route to HF | HF subtype |
|---|---|---|
| AS | Pressure overload → concentric LVH → stiff LV | HFpEF early → HFrEF late |
| AR chronic | Volume overload → eccentric LVH + dilation | HFrEF |
| MS | LA obstruction → pulmonary HTN → RVH | Right HF then biventricular |
| MR chronic | LV volume overload → LV dilation | HFrEF |

DCM → HFrEF (systolic dysfunction); HCM → HFpEF early (restrictive), HFrEF late (burnt-out).

See also: L3 — ESC 2023 HF management.

</details>

### Q: Why does HF subtype change the drug choice?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

🟨 ⭐ Per ESC 2023:
- **HFrEF / HFmrEF** → SGLT2i (Class I) + ACEi/ARNI + β-blocker + MRA + diuretics
- **HFpEF** → SGLT2i (Class I) + diuretic + **treat aetiology** (Class I)

If the aetiology is severe AS or HCM, treating the upstream lesion (AVR or septal reduction) IS the HF therapy.

🟥 ⚠️ Never prescribe standard HFrEF meds in HCM — vasodilators worsen LVOTO.

</details>

## Convergence 2 — Atrial Fibrillation

### Q: Which diseases cause AF, and which have special anticoagulation rules?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

| Disease | Anticoagulation rule |
|---|---|
| **MS (rheumatic)** | **Mandatory**; warfarin (valvular AF) |
| **HCM** | **Regardless of CHA₂DS₂-VASc** |
| DCM | Standard CHA₂DS₂-VASc-based |
| AS late | Standard CHA₂DS₂-VASc-based |

🟥 ⚠️ The two AF exceptions: valvular AF (rheumatic MS) and HCM + AF. Everything else follows CHA₂DS₂-VASc.

</details>

## Convergence 3 — Sudden Cardiac Death

### Q: Which diseases cause SCD, and in whom?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

- **HCM** — young adults, adolescents, athletes (leading cause in this group); ICD for primary/secondary prevention
- **AS** (severe untreated) — elderly with classic triad syncope/angina/dyspnoea; AVR
- **DCM** (advanced) — adults with low LVEF from VT; CRT-ICD
- **Acute severe MR** (post-MI) — cardiogenic shock; urgent surgery

🟦 🧠 Exam pattern: young + SCD on pitch → HCM. Elderly + syncope on exertion + ESM → severe AS. 3–7 days post-MI + shock + new murmur → papillary muscle rupture.

</details>

## Convergence 4 — LV Remodelling Patterns

### Q: What are the 4 remodelling patterns and how do you match them to disease?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

| Pattern | Disease | Driver |
|---|---|---|
| Concentric LVH | AS, HTN | Pressure overload |
| Eccentric + dilation | Chronic AR, chronic MR | Volume overload |
| Asymmetric (septal) LVH | HCM | Sarcomere mutation |
| Dilated thin-walled spherical | DCM | Myocardial injury |

Plus: LA dilation out of proportion to LV = MS.

🟦 🧠 Echo description → diagnosis. One of the fastest MCQ heuristics in this bundle.

</details>

## Convergence 5 — Functional Mitral Regurgitation

### Q: Primary vs functional MR — what's the trap?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Primary MR** — valve apparatus itself damaged (MVP, rheumatic, IE, chordal rupture).

**Functional MR** — valve apparatus structurally normal; another disease drives dysfunction:
- HCM → SAM → functional MR
- DCM → annular dilation from enlarged LV
- Ischaemic → papillary tethering

🟥 ⚠️ Don't replace a structurally normal MV. Treat the upstream disease — septal reduction in HCM usually resolves the MR.

See also: L2 — MR aetiology; L3 — SAM mechanism.

</details>

## Convergence 6 — Rheumatic Fever

### Q: Which valve lesions does rheumatic fever cause?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**All four** — AS, AR, MS, MR.

🟨 ⭐ **MS is the signature** — rheumatic is dominant cause worldwide.

Exam template: young adult from South Asia / Sub-Saharan Africa, history of childhood sore throats → rheumatic valve disease until proven otherwise (mitral > aortic).

Prevention = prompt Group A Strep treatment + aggressive rheumatic fever / IE treatment.

</details>

## Mixed-discrimination SBA block

### Q: SBA 1 — 68-year-old Bangladeshi man, childhood rheumatic fever. Dyspnoea + AF, CHA₂DS₂-VASc 2. Opening snap + mid-diastolic rumble. Anticoagulation approach?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Warfarin — rheumatic MS is valvular AF, high stroke risk.**

Not DOAC (generally avoided in severe rheumatic MS). Not aspirin. Not deferral.

</details>

### Q: SBA 2 — 28-year-old, tall, ectopia lentis, BP 170/52, early diastolic decrescendo murmur. Mechanism?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Aortic root dilation from connective tissue disease → valve leaflet non-coaptation.**

Marfan phenotype + wide PP + diastolic murmur = AR from root dilation.

</details>

### Q: SBA 3 — 17-year-old footballer, SCD on pitch. FH: father on ICD (HCM), paternal grandfather died at 49. Most important immediate action for family?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Cascade genetic testing + clinical screening (echo + ECG) of first-degree relatives.**

HCM is autosomal dominant. The case is explicitly built around the failure to screen the father's relatives.

</details>

### Q: SBA 4 — 74-year-old, exertional dyspnoea + syncope, BP 130/78, harsh crescendo-decrescendo systolic murmur, radiates to carotids, **softer** with Valsalva. Diagnosis?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Severe aortic stenosis.**

Softer with Valsalva = AS (reduced preload = less to eject). Louder with Valsalva would be HOCM. Carotid radiation = AS, not HOCM.

</details>

### Q: SBA 5 — 62-year-old, DCM (EF 30%) 5 months after anthracycline chemo. On diuretic only. Next medication per ESC 2023?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**SGLT2 inhibitor (dapagliflozin).** Class I first-line before ACEi.

Note: anthracycline-induced DCM as aetiology.

</details>

### Q: SBA 6 — 58-year-old, 4 days post-MI, sudden dyspnoea, pink frothy sputum, BP 82/54, soft new apical systolic murmur. Mechanism?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Papillary muscle rupture → acute severe MR.**

🟥 ⚠️ Murmur is soft because LA pressure rises rapidly, collapsing the gradient. Don't dismiss. Pulmonary oedema + shock post-MI = the picture.

</details>

### Q: SBA 7 — 45-year-old HCM, LVOT gradient 70 mmHg, severe exertional dyspnoea on max β-blocker, SAM + moderate functional MR, NYHA III. Next step?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Septal myectomy or alcohol septal ablation.**

Not ACEi / DHP CCB (vasodilators worsen LVOTO). Not MV replacement (MR is functional). Not more β-blocker (already failed).

</details>

### Q: SBA 8 — 72-year-old frail woman, severe symptomatic AS, life expectancy 5–7 years. Best approach?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**TAVR with bioprosthetic valve.**

- TAVR = less invasive, suits frail/elderly
- Bioprosthetic = no lifelong anticoag; lifespan matches remaining life

Mechanical = for younger patients. Balloon valvotomy = MS-specific.

</details>

### Q: SBA 9 — 26-year-old, 6 weeks postpartum, breathlessness + orthopnoea + ankle oedema. TTE: dilated LV, EF 30%. No prior cardiac history. Diagnosis + counselling?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Peripartum cardiomyopathy.** Future pregnancies carry significant recurrence risk and need specialist counselling.

</details>

### Q: SBA 10 — 36-year-old, non-obstructive HCM (IVS 20 mm), one NSVT on Holter, asymptomatic. Management plan?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Non-vasodilating β-blocker + ICD for primary prevention of SCD + cascade genetic screening of first-degree relatives.**

NSVT + HCM = arrhythmic risk. ICD indicated. β-blocker first-line. Family screening mandatory.

</details>

### Q: SBA 11 — 35-year-old marathon runner, FH of SCD, IVS 13 mm, deep Q waves + T-wave inversion on ECG, MYH7 mutation. Does he meet HCM criteria?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Yes.** 15 mm threshold drops to **13 mm** with FH or ECG abnormalities. He has both. MYH7 = classic HCM missense gain-of-function mutation.

</details>

### Q: SBA 12 — Which manoeuvre most rapidly discriminates AS from HOCM at the bedside?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Valsalva.** HOCM louder (↓ preload → smaller LV → worse LVOTO). AS softer (↓ preload → less to eject).

🟦 🧠 Dynamic murmur behaviour = bedside discriminator across this bundle.

</details>

### Q: SBA 13 — Why do β-blockers help severe MS but not severe AS?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**β-blockers prolong diastole**, giving more time for LA→LV filling across a narrowed MV.

AS obstruction is in systole, so lengthening diastole doesn't help; β-blockers risk dropping CO without benefit.

</details>

### Q: SBA 14 — 48-year-old with HCM + new AF, CHA₂DS₂-VASc 1. Anticoagulation?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Anticoagulate regardless of CHA₂DS₂-VASc score.**

The highest-yield HCM management trap.

</details>

### Q: SBA 15 — Echo: LV wall 17 mm, cavity at lower-normal size, aortic valve area 0.9 cm². Haemodynamic explanation?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Pressure overload → concentric LVH.**

AVA 0.9 cm² = severe AS. Thickened wall + preserved cavity = concentric. HCM would give asymmetric septum + FH — not stated here.

</details>

### Q: SBA 16 — 54-year-old, 30-year heavy drinking, progressive dyspnoea + bibasal crackles + bilateral ankle oedema. TTE: globally dilated LV, EF 28%, spherical shape, both ventricles affected. Diagnosis?

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

**Alcohol-related dilated cardiomyopathy.**

Long alcohol + global hypokinesis + biventricular involvement = classic alcoholic DCM pattern.

</details>

## Final reusable cross-lecture pattern

### Q: The 6-step SHD decision framework for any unseen stem.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

🟦 🧠 Sequence:

1. **Valve problem or muscle problem?** Murmur + specific sign → valve. LVH/dilation without murmur → CM. Both → CM causing functional valve disease.

2. **If valve:** which valve, which direction, stuck how? Systolic → AS/MR/HOCM; diastolic → AR/MS. Use radiation + pulse + dynamic manoeuvres.

3. **If muscle:** thick small cavity → HCM/pressure overload; thin dilated spherical → DCM; asymmetric septum + SAM → obstructive HCM.

4. **What is the remodelling telling you?** Concentric / eccentric / asymmetric / dilated — 4-way split → diagnosis.

5. **Downstream problem?** HF (classify by EF) / AF (check for HCM or MS exceptions) / SCD (young = HCM, elderly = AS, post-MI = acute MR).

6. **Correct intervention?** Stenosis → replace; regurg → repair; acute regurg → emergency surgery; DCM → SGLT2i + HF ladder; HCM → non-vasodilating β-blocker + septal reduction + ICD + cascade screen.

</details>

### Q: Top 6 traps that catch students in this bundle.

<details>
<summary>Reveal</summary>

🟥 ⚠️
1. **MR = most common overall; AS = most common requiring treatment.** Both true; stem selects.
2. **Acute severe MR murmur can be soft.** Post-MI shock + pulmonary oedema is the giveaway.
3. **Bicuspid aortic valve ≠ the tricuspid valve.** Bicuspid AV is the congenital defect.
4. **β-blockers help MS, not AS.** Diastolic vs systolic obstruction.
5. **HCM + AF = anticoagulate score-independent.** Highest-yield HCM trap.
6. **HCM's MR is functional.** Don't replace the valve — treat the LVOTO.

</details>
