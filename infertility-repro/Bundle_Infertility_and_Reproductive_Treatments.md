## Convergence 1 — Fertility needs gonadotrophins

### Q: L1 diagnosis vs L2 treatment — man with hypogonadotrophic hypogonadism?

> - Fertility wanted → hCG ± FSH
> - Fertility not wanted → testosterone
> - Applies to Kallmann, FHA, hyperprolactinaemia, hypopituitarism

### Q: L1 diagnosis vs L2 treatment — woman with hypogonadotrophic hypogonadism?

> - Fertility wanted → pulsatile GnRH or FSH (± cabergoline for hyperprolactinaemia)
> - Fertility not wanted → HRT

### Q: L1 diagnosis vs L2 treatment — PCOS?

> - Fertility wanted → lifestyle → metformin → letrozole
> - Fertility not wanted → COCP

### Q: L1 diagnosis vs L2 treatment — primary gonadal failure (Klinefelter, Turner, POI)?

> - Gonadotrophins won't help — the gonad itself has failed
> - Fertility → ICSI / donor gametes
> - Otherwise → testosterone (Klinefelter) or HRT (Turner, POI)

⚠️ Don't try hCG/FSH on a failed gonad. Klinefelter men need micro-TESE + ICSI; Turner women need donor eggs.

See: **L1 — Part A (three-level framework); L2 — Territories 1 and 2.**

## Convergence 2 — Unopposed oestrogen causes endometrial cancer

### Q: PCOS context — why unopposed E, how to oppose?

> - Chronic anovulation → no corpus luteum → no P → endometrium sees E alone
> - Oppose with COCP / Mirena / periodic progesterone 3–4×/year

### Q: HRT context — why unopposed E, how to oppose?

> - Exogenous E added to post-menopausal woman with uterus
> - Oppose with combined E + progestogen (oral or Mirena)

### Q: Trans man on T with intact uterus — why unopposed E, how to oppose?

> - Ovaries still make some E; no ovulation → no P
> - Oppose with progesterone to suppress menstrual bleeding
> - 15% otherwise develop hyperplasia

⭐ One rule: functioning endometrium meets oestrogen without progesterone → add progestogen.

See: **L1 — PCOS; L2 — HRT, trans men.**

## Convergence 3 — Same GnRH drug, opposite effect

### Q: Pulsatile GnRH — clinical use?

> - Pump, ~every 90 min
> - Stimulates pituitary → LH/FSH
> - Used in hypothalamic amenorrhoea

### Q: Continuous GnRH agonist — clinical use?

> - Long-acting preparation
> - Desensitises pituitary → shuts axis down
> - Used in transgender women and prostate cancer

🧠 Biology only responds to rhythm. Same molecule; delivery determines direction.

See: **L2 — Territories 2 and 7.**

## Convergence 4 — Amenorrhoea differential → treatment

### Q: ↓LH ↓FSH ↓E2 with anosmia — diagnosis and first-line if fertility?

> Kallmann syndrome → pulsatile GnRH (or FSH + LH).

### Q: ↓LH ↓FSH ↓E2 with low BMI/excess exercise — diagnosis and first-line?

> FHA → lifestyle → pulsatile GnRH → FSH.

### Q: ↓LH ↓FSH ↓E2 with ↑PRL — diagnosis and first-line?

> Hyperprolactinaemia → **cabergoline**.

### Q: ↓LH ↓FSH ↓E2 with pituitary mass — diagnosis and first-line?

> Hypopituitarism → gonadotrophin injections.

### Q: ↑LH:FSH + ↑androgens + PCO morphology — diagnosis and first-line?

> PCOS → letrozole (after lifestyle).

### Q: ↑LH ↑FSH ↓E2 under age 40 — diagnosis and first-line?

> POI → HRT + donor-egg IVF if fertility desired.

### Q: ↑LH ↑FSH ↓E2 with congenital stigmata — diagnosis and first-line?

> Turner → HRT + donor-egg IVF.

⭐ Most exam-transferable table in the bundle — know the pattern → diagnosis → drug flow cold.

See: **L1 — atlas cards; L2 — Territories 1 and 2.**

## Convergence 5 — Transdermal vs oral oestrogen

### Q: Oral oestrogen — profile?

> - Through liver (first-pass)
> - ↑clotting factors → ↑↑VTE
> - Preferred only if low risk and patient prefers tablets

### Q: Transdermal oestrogen — profile?

> - Direct to blood (bypasses liver)
> - Clotting factors unchanged → baseline VTE
> - Preferred if BMI >30, VTE risk, migraine history, trans women on high-dose E

See: **L2 — Territories 5 and 7.**

## The bundle method — seven-step pipeline

### Q: Step 1–2 — bloods and level?

> 1. Read bloods: LH, FSH, gonadal steroid, PRL
> 2. Place on three-level matrix:
>    - ↓↓↓ → hypothalamic or pituitary
>    - ↑↑↓ → gonadal
>    - ↑LH:FSH + ↑androgens + normal/↓E2 → PCOS

### Q: Step 3 — collapse differential with clinical context?

> - Anosmia → Kallmann
> - Low BMI/exercise → FHA
> - ↑PRL → hyperprolactinaemia
> - Tall + 47,XXY → Klinefelter
> - Short + webbed neck + 45,X → Turner
> - Postpartum haemorrhage → Sheehan's
> - Prior chemo → iatrogenic primary failure

### Q: Step 4 — fertility goal splits the tree how?

> - Hypogonadotrophic + fertility → gonadotrophins (hCG ± FSH in men; pulsatile GnRH or FSH ± LH in women)
> - Hypogonadotrophic + no fertility → sex steroid (T in men; MHT in women)
> - PCOS + fertility → lifestyle → metformin → letrozole
> - PCOS + no fertility → COCP

### Q: Step 4 — primary gonadal failure and hyperprolactinaemia branches?

> - Primary gonadal failure → ICSI / donor / accept + replace steroid
> - Hyperprolactinaemia → cabergoline regardless of fertility goal

### Q: Step 5–6 — safety rules (core)?

> - Progestogen if exogenous E + uterus
> - Transdermal if VTE risk
> - T needs Hct + PSA monitoring; never T for fertility
> - Migraine with aura → no COCP (any route)

### Q: Step 5–6 — safety rules (supporting)?

> - P450 inducers reduce hormonal efficacy
> - GnRH pulsatile ≠ continuous
> - Always pregnancy test first in a reproductive-age woman

### Q: Step 7 — discriminators that flip the answer?

> BMI, smoking, breastfeeding, current medications, age.

## Interleaved bundle SBAs

### Q: SBA 1 — 24F BMI 17, exercises 25h/week, 11mo amenorrhoea. LH 1.0, FSH 2.2, E2 low. Wants to conceive. First step?

> **B. Lifestyle modification.** FHA (L1) → ladder starts with lifestyle reversal (L2). Clomiphene/letrozole poor in HA (E2 already low). Pulsatile GnRH next if lifestyle fails.
>
> A) COCP · B) Lifestyle (↓exercise, gain weight) · C) Letrozole · D) Clomiphene · E) Pulsatile GnRH

### Q: SBA 2 — 31F 3y infertility, Rotterdam 3/3 PCOS, BMI 31, lost 4% without ovulation. Next step?

> **B. Letrozole** — first-line pharmacological in PCOS. Metformin alone is adjunctive.
>
> A) Clomiphene · B) Letrozole · C) Metformin alone · D) FSH injections · E) COCP

### Q: SBA 3 — 36M Kallmann on T gel 6y, now wants fertility. T 18 (on gel), LH + FSH undetectable. Change?

> **B.** Fertility needs gonadotrophins. T suppresses axis — stop. Clomiphene needs intact GnRH neurons (absent). Donor sperm premature.
>
> A) Continue T + clomiphene · B) Stop T; hCG; add FSH at 6 months if no response · C) Increase T dose · D) Donor sperm · E) Cabergoline

### Q: SBA 4 — 48F post-hysterectomy + BSO, severe hot flushes, BMI 34, prior DVT. Best HRT?

> **C. Transdermal E-only.** No uterus → no P needed. BMI 34 + prior DVT → avoid oral E.
>
> A) Oral combined continuous · B) Oral E-only · C) Transdermal E-only · D) Transdermal combined E + P · E) Vaginal E only

### Q: SBA 5 — 27F trans woman on 5 mg oral E. Calf pain + swelling, ↑D-dimer. Mechanism?

> **B.** Same mechanism as oral HRT VTE — first-pass → ↑clotting factors. Transdermal avoids.
>
> A) Progesterone hypercoagulability · B) First-pass metabolism of oral E → ↑clotting factors · C) T polycythaemia · D) GnRH agonist vasculitis · E) Anti-androgen thrombocytosis

### Q: SBA 6 — 35F 4 days post unprotected sex, on rifampicin, BMI 30. Best emergency contraception?

> **C. Copper IUD** — 5-day window, non-hormonal (rifampicin-resistant), BMI-independent, <1% failure.
>
> A) Levonorgestrel · B) Ulipristal · C) Copper IUD · D) POP · E) COCP

### Q: SBA 7 — 34M infertility. T 4, LH 14, FSH 22. Karyotype 47,XXY. Wife wants to conceive. Next step?

> **D.** Klinefelter = primary gonadal failure. Gonadotrophins won't help — gonad has failed. Micro-TESE + ICSI is the fertility route.
>
> A) hCG · B) Testosterone · C) Clomiphene · D) Micro-TESE + ICSI · E) Donor sperm

### Q: SBA 8 — 28F galactorrhoea, amenorrhoea, PRL 3200, LH/FSH low, E2 low, 1.2cm pituitary adenoma. First-line?

> **B. Cabergoline.** Dopamine agonist restores the axis and shrinks tumour. Surgery only if resistant or urgent mass effect.
>
> A) Transsphenoidal surgery · B) Cabergoline · C) Radiotherapy · D) Oestrogen · E) Letrozole

### Q: SBA 9 — 15F primary amenorrhoea, short stature, webbed neck, shield chest, poor breast development. LH 38, FSH 42, E2 low. Karyotype?

> **C. 45,X.** Turner syndrome — classic stigmata + hypergonadotrophic hypogonadism.
>
> A) 46,XX · B) 47,XXX · C) 45,X · D) 46,XY · E) 47,XXY

### Q: SBA 10 — 52F 14mo post-LMP, hot flushes, intact uterus, BMI 24, no VTE history. Simplest HRT?

> **B or C** — both acceptable. Normal BMI without VTE risk → oral combined sequential reasonable. C if any VTE concern or patient prefers patch. Must have P (uterus).
>
> A) Oral E-only · B) Oral combined sequential · C) Transdermal E + oral P (or Mirena) · D) Vaginal E · E) Tibolone

### Q: SBA 11 — 30F PCOS, not sexually active, 3 cycles/year, normal metabolic markers. Wants to reduce hirsutism. Best single option?

> **A. COCP.** Addresses three at once: regular withdrawal bleeds (endometrial protection); ↓LH → ↓androgens; ↑SHBG → ↓free androgen → hirsutism.
>
> A) COCP · B) Mirena IUS · C) Metformin · D) Spironolactone alone · E) Letrozole

### Q: SBA 12 — Letrozole — which feedback step does it pharmacologically leverage?

> **B. Kisspeptin neuron feedback by oestrogen.** Letrozole lowers E2 → reduced negative feedback onto kisspeptin (only site carrying ER in the axis) → ↑GnRH → ↑LH/FSH.
>
> A) GnRH release · B) Kisspeptin feedback by E2 · C) LH/FSH release · D) Follicular rupture · E) Corpus luteum P production

### Q: SBA 13 — 55F on continuous combined HRT 3y, previously amenorrhoeic, new vaginal bleeding. Next step?

> **C.** Breakthrough expected first few months; 3 years in with new bleeding = red flag for endometrial pathology.
>
> A) Reassure · B) Switch to sequential · C) Investigate for endometrial cancer (TVUS ± hysteroscopy ± biopsy) · D) Stop HRT · E) Increase P

### Q: SBA 14 — 33M infertility, small testes, T 14 (normal), LH 2, FSH 3, PRL 4500. Single most useful next investigation?

> **B. MRI pituitary.** Very high PRL + hypogonadotrophic → likely prolactinoma. MRI localises lesion and assesses mass effect.
>
> A) Karyotype · B) MRI pituitary · C) Repeat semen · D) Morning cortisol · E) Testicular biopsy

### Q: SBA 15 — 25F wants most effective reversible contraception, no daily/weekly action, normal bleeding preserved. Best option?

> **C. Copper IUD** — <1% failure, non-hormonal, preserves ovulation/cycle (though bleeding can be heavier). D/E: highly effective but alter bleeding patterns.
>
> A) COCP · B) Depot · C) Copper IUD · D) Mirena IUS · E) Subdermal implant

### Q: SBA 16 — 45,X woman with Turner asks about fertility. Most accurate counselling?

> **D. Donor eggs + IVF.** Turner = primary gonadal failure — stimulation useless. Pre-pregnancy cardiac assessment essential (aortic root + coarctation risks).
>
> A) Natural conception · B) Letrozole first-line · C) hCG · D) Donor eggs + IVF; cardiac assessment first · E) Fertility impossible

> 1. Bloods: LH, FSH, gonadal steroid, PRL
> 2. Level: hypothalamic, pituitary, PCOS, gonadal

> 5. Goal: fertility wanted or not
> 6. Drug by level × goal: gonadotrophins / sex steroid / letrozole / cabergoline / COCP / HRT / ICSI
